René Favaloro

Argentinean educator and heart surgeon

Here's a time line of Dr. René Favaloro:

  • 1923 - Born in La Plata, Argentina.
  • 1936 - He began his studies at Rafael Hernández National School.
  • 1949 - Graduated from the Rafael Hernandez National College.
  • 1962 - He went to the Cleveland Clinic.
  • 1967 - He studied the possibility of using the saphenous vein in coronary surgery, practicing his ideas in May of that year. The standardization of this technique, called the bypass or myocardial revascularization surgery, was the main work of his career which gave him international prestige, since the procedure radically changed the history of coronary disease.
  • 1970 - He edited a book called Surgical Treatment.
  • 1971 - Return to Argentina to operate at the Guemes sanatorium in the Federal Capital.
  • 1975 - He founded the Favaloro Foundation for this purpose together with other collaborators.
  • 1980 - Favaloro created the research lab.
  • 1992 - The Institute of Cardiology was inaugurated in Buenos Aires.
  • 1998 - La creacion de la Universidad Favaloro.
  • 2000 - Rene committed suicide at the age of 77 due to the economic crisis, the Favaloro Foundation was in debt for US$ 75 million.

I would cease to exist if I did not have before me challenges that involve above all things, contributing within and outside my profession to the ethical development of man."
